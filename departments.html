Our Departments

Choose from three specialized departments designed to prepare you for a successful career in the technology industry.

B.Sc. Computer Science

Department of Computer Science

The Department of Computer Science offers a comprehensive program focused on the systematic approach to software development. Students learn to design, develop, test, and maintain software applications using industry-standard methodologies and tools.

Program Overview

Our curriculum emphasizes the software development lifecycle, from requirements gathering to deployment and maintenance. Students gain practical experience through team projects, internships, and collaboration with industry partners.

4 Years Duration
NUC Accredited

Industry Methodologies

Learn the methodologies used by leading tech companies worldwide.

Agile & Scrum

Iterative development with continuous feedback and adaptation.

DevOps

Integrating development and operations for faster delivery.

Software Architecture

Designing scalable and maintainable software systems.

Clean Code

Writing readable, maintainable, and efficient code.

B.Sc. Cyber Security

Department of Cyber Security

The Department of Cyber Security offers a cutting-edge undergraduate program designed to prepare students for the rapidly growing field of information security. With cyber threats becoming increasingly sophisticated, our program equips students with the skills needed to protect digital assets and infrastructure.

Program Overview

Our program covers essential aspects of cybersecurity, including network security, ethical hacking, digital forensics, and cyber defense. Students gain hands-on experience through security labs and real-world simulations.

4 Years Duration
NUC Accredited

Focus Areas

Our program covers key areas in modern cybersecurity.

Network Security

Protecting computer networks from unauthorized access and attacks.

Ethical Hacking

Identifying vulnerabilities through authorized penetration testing.

Digital Forensics

Investigating cyber crimes and recovering digital evidence.

Cyber Defense

Developing strategies to defend against cyber threats.

Course Structure

Year 1

  • Introduction to Cybersecurity
  • Security Fundamentals
  • Computer Networks Basics
  • Operating Systems

Year 2

  • Network Security
  • Cryptography
  • Database Security
  • Web Security

Year 3

  • Ethical Hacking
  • Digital Forensics
  • Security Operations
  • Cloud Security

Year 4

  • Advanced Threat Detection
  • Final Year Project
  • Security Governance
  • Electives

Why Study Cyber Security?

In today's interconnected world, cybersecurity is more important than ever. Organizations across all sectors need skilled professionals who can protect their digital assets, customer data, and critical infrastructure. The demand for cybersecurity experts continues to grow globally, offering excellent career prospects and competitive salaries.

Career Paths

Security Analyst
Security Architect
Security Consultant
Incident Response Analyst
Penetration Tester
Forensic Investigator
Chief Information Security Officer
Malware Analyst

Admission Requirements

  • Five O'Level credits including Mathematics and English Language
  • Credit in Physics or any Science subject
  • Minimum JAMB score as specified by the university
  • Pass in JAMB UTME with relevant subject combinations
